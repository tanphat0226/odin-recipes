Phở

Ingredients

  • 500g beef bones (for broth)
  • 300g beef brisket or flank
  • 200g beef sirloin or eye of round, thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, halved
  • 1 piece ginger (about 5cm), halved
  • 3 star anise
  • 3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 cardamom pod (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
  • Salt and rock sugar to taste
  • 500g phở rice noodles (flat rice noodles)
  • Fresh herbs: Thai basil, cilantro, scallions
  • Bean sprouts
  • Lime wedges
  • Thinly sliced onion
  • Sliced chili or chili sauce (optional)
  • Hoisin sauce and sriracha for serving

Steps

  1. Char the onion and ginger over an open flame or under a broiler until slightly blackened, then rinse with water.
  2. In a large pot, add beef bones and cover with water. Bring to a boil and blanch bones for 5 minutes. Drain and rinse bones to remove impurities.
  3. Refill pot with fresh water, add bones, charred onion, ginger, and spices (star anise, cloves, cinnamon, cardamom, coriander, fennel).
  4. Simmer broth gently for at least 6 hours (preferably 8–10 hours) to develop deep flavor. Skim foam regularly.
  5. About 1 hour before finishing, add brisket to the pot and cook until tender. Remove brisket, slice thinly, and set aside.
  6. Season broth with salt and rock sugar to taste.
  7. Cook phở noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse with cold water.
  8. To serve: place noodles in bowls, top with sliced brisket and raw sirloin slices.
  9. Ladle hot broth over the noodles and beef to cook the raw slices.
  10. Garnish with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, sliced onion, lime wedges, chili, hoisin sauce, and sriracha as desired.
